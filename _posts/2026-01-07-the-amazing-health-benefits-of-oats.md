<br>
<p class="show-intro">
oats, or 'avena sativa', are one of the most nourishing and beneficial foods you can incorporate into your diet.
</p>
<br>
<p class="show-intro">
unrefined oats (which make up the majority of commercially available oats) are a whole grain. this means they retain all three parts of the grain kernel: the bran, germ, and endosperm. each part of the grain contributes essential elements. the bran layer is rich in fibre and b vitamins, which support digestion and energy metabolism. the germ contains healthy oils and important nutrients, and the starchy endosperm provides sustained energy along with b vitamins, iron, and fibre.
</p>
<br>
<p class="show-intro">
due to this structure, oats have a well-balanced nutritional composition. they supply complex carbohydrates that fuel the body steadily, as opposed to leading to drastic energy spikes and crashes.
</p>
<br>
<p class="show-intro">
for a grain, oats are also a good source of plant-based protein. this makes them particularly beneficial to vegans (me!!) or vegetarians.
</p>
<br>
<p class="show-intro">
oats also contain large amounts of essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidant compounds. this dense concentration of nutrients has led to them being often-described as one of the most nutrient-dense foods on the planet.
</p>
<br>
<p class="show-intro">
one of the most important components of oats is a soluble fibre called beta-glucan. this fibre plays a major role in many of oatsâ€™ health benefits. beta-glucan helps reduce blood glucose and insulin response after meals. by forming a thick gel in the digestive tract, it also slows the absorption of glucose into the bloodstream, which supports stable blood sugar levels.
</p>
<br>
<p class="show-intro">
this same fibre contributes to the regulation of type 2 diabetes AND supports heart health by helping to lower cholesterol levels. even further, it promotes the growth of beneficial bacteria in the gut, improving digestive health and supporting a healthy microbiome. fibre might be the best thing in the world.
</p>
<br>
<p class="show-intro">
due to their high fibre content, oats are also extremely filling. this sense of fullness can naturally support weight management if this is a goal of yours.
</p>
<br>
<p class="show-intro">
oats provide a source of non-heme iron, the form of iron found in plant foods. though this type of iron is less easily absorbed on its own, there is a simple way to change this...
</p>
<br>
<p class="show-intro">
pairing oats with orange juice can significantly improve iron absorption. orange juice is rich in vitamin c, which increases the bioavailability of non-heme iron when consumed at the same time. </p>
